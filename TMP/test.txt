Taking in the lavender crown’s familiar scent, the wolf put her nose to the baby. The babe raised her arms to the wolf, her single hand clasped in a fist. The wolf opened her jaws and tenderly licked the child’s face. 

Then she lifted the babe in the snow, guarding her from the night. The pack at once surrounding them for protection.
As the wolves howls continued to ring through the glade a barely noticeable doorway opened and a band of fierce men and women stepped from the dark into the moonlit clearing, drawing their daggers in readiness.
